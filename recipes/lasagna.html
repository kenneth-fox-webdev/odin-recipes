Lasagna

Recipe Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish known for its rich layers of pasta, savory meat sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and melty mozzarella. Believed to have originated from the Emilia-Romagna region of Italy, this comforting dish has become a beloved staple in kitchens around the world. Traditional lasagna combines sheets of pasta with a hearty Bolognese sauce, typically made with ground beef or pork simmered in tomatoes, garlic, onions, and Italian herbs. The layers are nestled between smooth cheese fillings, topped with grated Parmesan, and baked until bubbling and golden brown. The balance of textures and flavors—from the slightly crisp top layer to the tender, saucy center—makes lasagna both satisfying and indulgent.

While many variations exist, the essential spirit of lasagna lies in its slow-cooked richness and generous layering. It’s a dish often reserved for special occasions or family gatherings, not only because of its hearty nature but also due to the love and patience required in its preparation. Each layer builds upon the next, blending flavors and creating a cohesive and deeply flavorful meal. Whether served with a side of garlic bread or a simple salad, lasagna is a meal that brings people together and leaves a lasting impression of warmth and home-cooked comfort.

Lasagna Ingredients

For the Meat Sauce:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2–3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 500g (1 lb) ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 800g (28 oz) crushed tomatoes (canned or fresh)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup red wine (optional, but adds depth)
  • Fresh parsley or basil, chopped (optional garnish)

For the Cheese Filling:

  • 450g (1 lb) ricotta cheese (or cottage cheese)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Other Ingredients:

  • 9–12 lasagna noodles (regular or oven-ready)
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Additional Parmesan for topping

Steps to Make Lasagna

  1. Prepare the Meat Sauce

    In a large saucepan or skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for 5–6 minutes until soft and translucent. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for 1 more minute until fragrant. Add the ground beef, breaking it apart with a spoon, and cook until browned and no longer pink. Drain excess fat if necessary.

  2. Simmer the Sauce

    Stir in the tomato paste and let it cook for 1–2 minutes to deepen the flavor. Add the crushed tomatoes, sugar, oregano, basil, red wine (if using), salt, and pepper. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer uncovered for 30–45 minutes, stirring occasionally until thickened. Adjust seasoning to taste. Optional: Add chopped fresh herbs for a burst of flavor.

  3. Cook the Noodles (if not using oven-ready)

    While the sauce simmers, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and lay them flat on a towel or parchment paper to prevent sticking.

  4. Prepare the Cheese Filling

    In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, egg, grated Parmesan, chopped parsley, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Mix until smooth and set aside.

  5. Assemble the Lasagna

    Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish. Add a layer of noodles, then spread a portion of the ricotta cheese mixture over the noodles. Sprinkle with a bit of mozzarella, then spoon over more meat sauce. Repeat the layers—noodles, ricotta, mozzarella, meat sauce—until all ingredients are used, finishing with a generous layer of sauce and the remaining mozzarella and Parmesan on top.

  6. Bake

    Cover the dish with foil (tenting it so it doesn’t touch the cheese) and bake for 25 minutes. Then remove the foil and bake uncovered for another 20–25 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbly. Let it rest for at least 15 minutes before slicing to help the layers set.

  7. Serve and Enjoy

    Garnish with fresh parsley or basil if desired. Serve with a side salad or garlic bread for a complete meal. Leftovers can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 4 days or frozen for longer storage.
