Chicken and Dumplings

Recipe Description

Chicken and dumplings is the ultimate comfort food—a warm, hearty dish that brings together tender chicken, flavorful broth, and soft, pillowy dumplings. It’s a dish that feels like a hug in a bowl, perfect for cold days, family gatherings, or any time you crave something filling and homey. This recipe simplifies the process without sacrificing the classic flavors you know and love.

In this version, you’ll simmer chicken with aromatic vegetables to build a rich, savory broth. The dumplings are easy to mix and cook right in the same pot, soaking up all the delicious flavor. Whether you're new to this Southern staple or grew up with it, this recipe brings all the coziness of a home-cooked classic to your table with minimal effort and maximum satisfaction.

Ingredients (Serves 4–6)

For the soup:

  • 1 tbsp olive oil or butter
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 1 lb (450g) cooked shredded chicken (rotisserie or poached)
  • 1 tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • ½ cup heavy cream or whole milk (optional for creaminess)
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley (for garnish)

For the dumplings:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ cup milk
  • 2 tbsp butter, melted

Steps to Make Chicken and Dumplings

  1. Sauté the vegetables

    In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil or butter over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and celery. Cook for 5–6 minutes until softened. Add the garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  2. Build the soup base

    Pour in the chicken broth and stir in the salt, pepper, thyme, and bay leaf. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes to blend flavors.

  3. Add chicken and cream

    Stir in the cooked shredded chicken and the optional heavy cream or milk if using. Let the soup continue to simmer while you prepare the dumplings.

  4. Make the dumplings

    In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt. Add the milk and melted butter and stir just until combined—a thick, sticky dough will form.

  5. Add dumplings to the pot

    Drop spoonfuls of dough (about 1 tablespoon each) directly onto the simmering soup. Cover the pot with a lid and simmer for 15 minutes without lifting the lid. The steam will cook the dumplings until they’re soft and fluffy.

  6. Finish and serve

    Remove the bay leaf. Taste the broth and adjust seasoning if needed. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve hot.
