Cheeseburger

Recipe Description

Few things are as satisfying as a classic homemade cheeseburger fresh off the grill or skillet. Juicy, seasoned beef, perfectly melted cheese, and a toasted bun create a comforting, savory bite that’s both nostalgic and endlessly customizable. Whether you're cooking for a family dinner, weekend cookout, or just craving something hearty and indulgent, this cheeseburger hits all the right notes with minimal fuss and maximum flavor.

This recipe focuses on crafting a simple yet delicious cheeseburger with ingredients you likely already have at home. You’ll learn how to season and shape the patties for optimal juiciness, how to get that perfect cheese melt, and how to build a burger with classic toppings like lettuce, tomato, and onion. Feel free to adjust the toppings and sauces to suit your taste—this burger serves as the ideal base for all your cheeseburger experiments.

Ingredients (Makes 4 burgers)

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef (80/20 lean-to-fat ratio is ideal)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp garlic powder (optional)
  • 1 tsp onion powder (optional)
  • 4 slices of cheddar cheese (or cheese of choice)
  • 4 hamburger buns
  • 1 tbsp butter (for toasting buns)
  • Lettuce leaves
  • 1 large tomato, sliced
  • 1 small red or white onion, thinly sliced
  • Pickles (optional)
  • Ketchup, mustard, or mayonnaise (to taste)

Steps for Cheeseburger Recipe

  1. Prepare the patties

    In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef with salt, pepper, and optional garlic/onion powder. Gently mix with your hands—avoid overmixing to keep the burgers tender. Divide the mixture into 4 equal portions and form into patties about ¾ inch thick. Press a small dimple into the center of each patty with your thumb to prevent puffing during cooking.

  2. Toast the buns

    Heat a skillet or griddle over medium heat. Spread a small amount of butter on the cut sides of each bun. Place the buns cut side down on the skillet until golden brown (1–2 minutes). Remove and set aside.

  3. Cook the patties

    Increase the heat to medium-high. Place the patties on the hot skillet or grill. Cook for 3–4 minutes on the first side, then flip. After flipping, add a slice of cheese to each patty and cover the pan with a lid (or close the grill) to help the cheese melt. Cook for another 3–4 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 160°F (71°C) for well-done.

  4. Assemble the burgers

    Spread condiments on the toasted buns as desired. Layer the lettuce, tomato, onion, and pickles (if using) on the bottom bun. Place the cooked patty with melted cheese on top of the toppings, then crown with the top bun.

  5. Serve

    Serve immediately with your favorite sides—fries, chips, or a fresh salad. Enjoy your homemade cheeseburger masterpiece!
